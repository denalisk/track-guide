So you want to be a programmer...

These days, it seems like everyone and their grandmother need in-house developers,

creating a great job market for people with the right skills. You're smart, personable, and a go-getter, so you want a piece of the action. But you don't want to end up as someone's code monkey, chained to a keyboard pounding out menial lines of commands to your computer overlords. When you sell your labor for money, you want to revel in each day's accomplishments and be recognized for your cleverness.

Well, put that world-beater nature to work, and use this simple guide to get yourself a bit of the American techno-dream.

Specialty versus Utility

What do you prefer?

Jack-of-all-trades, master of none

?

The generalist spreads their knowledge out, without concentrating it in any one place. They may not know the most about any subject, but they know enough about most anything to get the job done.

Specialist, master at home, stranger away

?

The specialist strives to push the realm of knowledge in their concentration. They are the person that people come to with questions about their specialty, but they likewise must go elsewhere to accomplish things outside their scope.

Form versus Function

Which do you value more?

Function

?

Function, efficiency, a straight-to-the-point approach to life. Who cares if it looks like a hodge-podge skeleton glued together by a bunch of colorblind elves as long as it's faster, stronger, smarter, tougher?

Form

?

Form, grace, simplicity in comprehension. There's no point to making a better mousetrap if it's so cruel and ugly looking that no one wants to use it.

Work Environment

What company type suits you best?

Small, dynamic, in flux

?

Smaller, newer companies still finding their way in the corporate world frequently offer workers diverse and changing responsibilities, as a smaller workforce frequently means that employees must wear many hats.

Large, stable, clearly defined

?

Larger, established companies with needs for developers usually have well-defined dimensions for roles a developer could be hired into, and offer much more stable employment opportunities to the clever and able.

Relationship with Information

What do you like to do with valuable informaiton?

Organize, catalog, and store it

?

All developers work with data and information. An essential component of dealing with data is how it is stored and organized, because no matter how much data you gather, if it's just hoarded in piles in a garage it's almost impossible to use efficiently.

Process, arrange, and communicate it

?

Computer programs are only as useful as the information they provide, and presenting that information to other people in a comprehensible manner is critical to making a program that is actually useful.

Portability versus Capability

Which characteristic do you value more?

Portability

?

When it comes down to it, you value the availability of something more than you value it's inherent abilities. If it's not accessible to you when you need it, what's the point?

Capability

?

Things should be valued based on their immediate utility, and more valuable things are simply more capable, convenience be damned.
